Homelessness in the United States

By Caroline Abel, Charles Onesti, and Emma Ton

Despite having the world's largest economy with a 2020 GDP of 23 trillion dollars, there are more than 580,000 people experiencing homelessness in the U.S. on any given day.

Homelessness by the Numbers

This chart displays the number of homeless peole in various categories using representative symbols of 5000. In other words, each dot represents 5000 people. People in a Emergency Shelter are those given temporary shelter when in economic or domestic distress. They are provided short term stability and are not forced to sign a lease or living agreement.

In Transitional Housing, people are given temporary shelter for 6 to 24 months and are often supported in finding employment. Select a state or all states in the dropdown above and scroll through the years to see the dots move over time.

Gender and Race Breakdown of the U.S. Homeless Population in 2021

Policies and historical events can have an effect on homelessness

Recent Policies and Events and their Effect on Overall Homelessness

The circles on the timeline above show the relative size of total homelessness in that year. As we can see from this visualization, overall homelessness numbers have not changed substantially over the years despite new policies and events.

Despite the policies in place, homelessness is a persistent problem. It takes a larger movement combining the efforts from both the government and the public. Our goal is to raise awareness about the homelessness problem to the public.

What Can You Do?

As we have seen, thousands of people across the country are homeless with limited help in sight.

Here are some things you can do to help:

  • Volunteer at a soup kitchen, homeless shelter, or charities involved in helping the homeless.
  • Fundraise for charities helping the homeless
  • Donate clothes and food.
  • Educate yourself and others about the homeless population and the homelessness issue.
  • Contact your representative to advocate for policies that help the homeless!
